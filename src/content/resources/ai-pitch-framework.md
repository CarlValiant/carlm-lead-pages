## What's Inside

In Q1 2026, over 80% of all venture capital went to AI startups. That left roughly $12 billion for every other founder — competing in a shrinking pool. This guide shows you how to move into the 80%.

You'll get:

- **The capital shift breakdown** — what PitchBook's Q1 2026 data actually means for non-AI founders
- **The 3 AI angles** that work for any business model: ops automation, industry-trained models, and AI-driven acquisition
- **Weak vs. strong examples** of the AI paragraph VCs want to see in your first three slides
- **A pre-pitch checklist** to make sure your AI angle is specific, defensible, and tied to real metrics

## Who This Is For

This guide is for founders actively raising a seed or Series A round who aren't building a pure-play AI company but need to speak credibly about AI in their pitch. If your last five VC meetings ended without a second call, this is likely why.

It's also useful for any founder who's ever said "we leverage AI to enhance our platform" — and wants to replace that line with something that actually lands.
